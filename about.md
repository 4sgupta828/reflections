I write about **agentic system design** with a production-first lens.

I am interested in the gap between a workflow that looks strong in isolation and a system that can be trusted in a messy, changing distributed environment. That is where state, uncertainty, observability, tooling, and policy matter more than any single prompt or benchmark.

I have worked on large-scale platforms at Tubi, Argo AI, Netflix, and Microsoft, building infrastructure and debugging systems for personalization, autonomous vehicle data, and high-scale backend services.

This blog captures my thinking on the engineering required to make non-deterministic systems inspectable, debuggable, and reliable over time, especially when AI workflows need to behave like **causal execution graphs** rather than opaque chains of calls.
